About 71 percent of the surface of the Earth is covered by the ocean, a vast body of saltwater. Oceanographers and nations around the world have traditionally classified the planet's ocean into four separate regions: the Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, and Arctic oceans. Treasures that were formerly only owned by French kings and queens were made available to the highest bidder in 1887. The Third Republic government created a case to get rid of the gems by branding them frivolous, thus ignoring the historical significance of the collection. From a position of authority, a deputy proclaimed: "A democracy that is sure of itself and confident in the future has a duty to rid itself of these objects of luxury, devoid of usefulness and moral worth."

The Russian word "kokoshnik" is translated. In the past, it was used to describe a headgear worn by married Russian women in specific regions of the nation. The size and shape of these classic kokoshniks varied slightly. Some of them were enormous, as seen in the image on the left, while others were smaller. However, kokoshniks generally had the same fundamental crescent shape. Many of them had ribbons fastening them at the back of the head.

For 300 years, Russia was controlled by the Romanov family. The finest jewelers in the world created some of the most intriguing crown jewels in history for the Romanovs during this time. The global leaders and ambassadors from Europe who visited the Russian royal court were deeply impressed by the superb bracelets, tiaras, diadems, crowns, rings, earrings, and brooches.
